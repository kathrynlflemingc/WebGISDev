Kate's M.Sc. Fieldwork Trip: July 2017

My M.Sc. in Earth Sciences focused on the study of a caldera complex in central Nevada. The goal of the project was to determine a magma source for the caldera; either a shallow crustal melt or a deep mantle melt. This was accomplished by conducting isotope geochemistry research using Pb, Sr, and Nd isotopes. However, the first step of our project was to collect rock samples from the caldera and its surrounding areas. In addition, other main goals of my fieldwork were to groundtruth existing mapping of the Underdown Caldera Complex and verify the existance of outflow tuff outside of the caldera. Together with my supervisor, we travelled from Carleton University to Reno, Nevada, making stops in Chicago and San Francisco along the way. From Reno we drove along Highway 50, which is often referred to as "the loneliest road in America", to reach our "base camp" in Austin, Nevada.

2017 Sampling Locations

On this trip, the main focus of our sampling was on outflow tuffs (tuffs which flowed outside of the caldera walls) from the Underdown Caldera Complex located in the Shoshone Mountain Range. Therefore, our sampling took place across a wide swath of the state, as can be seen on the map below. Each point represents a location where a sample was collected. Markers are colour coded based on the geological unit that the sample belongs to, which can be viewed when clicking on the marker.

2016 Sampling Locations

Additional samples used in this project were collected in 2016 by Kim Klausen, Brian Cousens, and Christopher Henry. In 2016, sampling of the intracaldera units of the Underdown Caldera was prioritized over sampling of the outflow tuffs. This included sampling of the Underdown Tuff, the Bonita Canyon Formation, the Unnamed Tuff, the Pablo Formation and associated rhyolitic intrusions. The Underdown Canyon in the North Shoshone Mountains was sampled extensively. Sampling locations are shown on the map below.
